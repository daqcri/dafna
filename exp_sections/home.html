This study compares 12 methods from the literature: TruthFinder, Cosine, 2-Estimates and 3-Estimates, LTM, MLE, Depen with its extensions: Accu, AccuSim and AccuNoDep, SimpleLCA and GuessLCA.

The goal of our study is:

  1. To provide a clear explanation of each algorithm, and allow comparison of their properties by using common notation, terminology, experimental set-ups, data sets, and test cases.
  2. To provide reference implementations of these algorithms against which future algorithms can be compared, new data sets can be analyzed, and on top of which algorithms for different problems or applications can be built, and finally,
  3. To perform a thorough experimental evaluation of the algorithms over a variety of data sets and report their performance and quality in practice for a wide spectrum of parameter settings.

Although there have been other comparisons of these algorithms on real-world data sets (e.g., here), they did not study parameter settings, repeatability, and convergence of the algorithms and did not test them extensively for a wide range of truth discovery scenarios. The experimental framework we developed for comparing the methods is a novel contribution to the field, so that others can use and extend it for benchmarking as well as parameter setting and tuning of new truth discovery algorithms.
